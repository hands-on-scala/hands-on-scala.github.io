Why should we care about pattern matching? Pattern matching is a quite commonly used “programming pattern” in functional languages, because it fits nicely into the “functional way of thinking”, and it is quite powerful and handy as a tool. It’s somewhat similar to a sequence of if / else statements or to the switch statement of Java or C, but it is much more powerful, because it lets you form more complex conditions for matching, even for the inside of an object.
